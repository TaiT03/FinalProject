Fruits & Vegetables

Here you will find all of the necessary information about fruits & vegetables. You will also find some things they are useful for aside from eating.

Fruits

There are 7 different categories of fruits: apples/pears, berries, citrus, melons, stone fruit, tomoatoes/avacadoes, tropical/exotic.
Here you will find a break down on exactly what each catgeory is and the types of fruits that are in them.

Apples/Pears

Apple:
Apples are considered to be nutrient-packed fruits. They offer vitamin E which is a fat-soluble antioxidant; vitamin B1 (or thiamine) which is needed for growth development; and vitamin B6 which is essential for brain development. Apples have many other great health benefits when you eat them such as lowering your risk for heart disease and diabetes. Apples are known to help against certain types of cancers such as lung, breast and digestive tract cancer. They are also good for gut health, which means they give your gut the bacteria it needs to sustain itself. Aside from eating them, you can use apples in many other ways such as ripening green tomatoes. You can do this by placing 5-6 green tomatoes in a bag with an apple or two (make sure you keep a ratio of 5-6 tomatoes per 1-2 apples for efficient ripening!!). Another way you can use apples is by including them in your workout! Place an apple in between your thighs while you do crunches, as this helps strengthen your core.

Pear:
There are almost 100 different types of pears that are grown worldwide. The most popular types are Barlett, Bosc, and D'Anjou. Pears are very beneficial to your health as they have flavanoid antioxidants, which means that they decrease your risk of diseases such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, breast cancer, & ovarian cancer. They have other compounds in them as well which are shown to fight against other cancers in the bladder, stomach, or lungs. Pears contain vitamin C, which helps keeps cells clean and healthy and help wounds heal; and vitamin K, which helps the body produce blood clots & helps wounds heal. They can be a source of potassium which is considered to be an electrolyte, and copper which is needed in the body to help form red blood cells. With them being packed with water and fiber, pears are considered to be a weight loss friendly food. Aside from eating them, pears can also be used for making a natural soap or body wash that will help keep your skin clear.

Berries

Strawberry:
Strawberries have many different health benefits. They offer different vitamins, such as vitamin C, which is needed for your immune system and skin health; vitamin B9, which is needed for tissue growth and cell function; vitamin B6, which is essential for brain development, and keeping the immune and nervous systems healthy; vitamin K, which helps the body with blood clotting and healing wounds; and vitamin E, which is good for vision and health. Strawberries contain a lot of necessary minerals as well, such as potassium, manganese, iron, copper, magnesium, and phosphorus. All of these minerals are responsible for forming connective tissues inside the body, maintaining fluid levels inside cells, helping make red blood cells, helping keep the immune system healthy, and energy reproduction. You can also use strawberries to make a strawberry face mask, as it has salicylic acid, which helps with acne and clearing up the skin.

Blueberry:
Blueberries are a good snack choice or addition to your meal as they are low in calories, but are very rich in nutrients. Some of the nutrients offered by blueberries are: vitamin C, which is needed for your immune system and skin health; and vitamin K, which helps the body with blood clotting and healing wounds. Blueberries also contain manganese and potassium. These minerals are necessary for forming connective tissues inside the body, maintaining fluid levels inside cells, and forming bones. Including blueberries in your diet, whether by eating or by drinking, has also been shown to help increase your body's antioxidant levels. This is necessary for your body to fight off many illnesses and diseases. Because they are antioxidant rich, blueberries are shown to help the brain maintain proper function and improve memory. If you have kids or want to do a fun activity with blueberries, you can also use them to dye eggs!

Raspberry:
Raspberries are a great addition to your diet as they are packed with plenty of nutrients and minerals. They have vitamin B6, which is needed for normal brain development, as well as keeping the nervous and immune systems healthy. Also, raspberries have vitamin E, which is needed for vision, and vitamin K which helps the body with blood clotting and healing wounds. Some of the minerals that can be found in raspberries are potassium, manganese, iron, copper, magnesium, and phosphorus. These minerals are very important to human health as they are needed for maintaining fluid levels inside cells, forming connective tissues inside the body, helping make red blood cells, helping keep the immune system healthy, and energy reproduction. You can include raspberries in your diet by eating them fresh out of the garden, baking them in a pie, making ice cream, and making smoothies or lemonade.

Blackberry:
Blackberries can be beneficial to your diet. They are packed with vitamin C, as this is crucial for bone formation, connective tissue repair, and blood vessels. In addition to this, vitamin C is known to help you heal wounds, reduce molecules released by toxins (free radicals), absorb iron, and prevent scurvy. Blackberries also have vitamin E, which is needed for body health and vision; and vitamin K which helps the body with blood clotting and healing wounds. You may also find minerals like calcium and manganese in blackberries. These minerals are responsible for blood clotting, helping muscles to contract, regulating normal heart rhythms and nerve function, and form connective tissue and bones. Blackberries also have antioxidants and brain boosting polyphenols. Here are some of the ways you can prepare blackberries: blackberry jam, blackberry pie, blackberry juice, and even blackberry liquor.

Cranberry
Cranberries are a very healthy fruit, as they are nearly 90% water, with the rest being necessary minerals and nutrients for the body. While it is beneficial to include them in your diet, they will help your body in a variety of ways. First, cranberries are filled with vitamin C, which is essential for the maintenance of your skin, muscles, and bones; vitamin E, which is a fat soluble antioxidant; and vitamin K1, which is needed for blood clotting. You will also be repleninshing minerals like manganese, which is essential for growth, metabolism, and your body's antioxidant system; and copper, which helps your body make red blood cells and keeps nerve cells and your immune system healthy. You can use cranberries as decorations during the holidays, fresh or dry, but will definitely last longer if they are dried out.

Boysenberry:
While eating these delightful fruits, you will be repleninshing your body, as they are rich in minerals and nutrients. Boysenberries have vitamin C, which is essential for the maintenance of your skin, muscles, and bones; vitamin E which is good for vision and health; vitamin K which helps the body with blood clotting and healing wounds; and the B vitamins and folate, which aid our body in multiple cellular functions, such as the break down of carbohydrates and the transporting nutrients throughout the body. Some of the nutrients that are found in boysenberries are manganese, magnesium, phosphorus, copper, iron, potassium, and calcium. These minerals are responsible for forming connective tissues inside the body, maintaining fluid levels inside cells, helping make red blood cells, helping keep the immune system healthy, and energy reproduction.

Citrus

Lemon:
Lemons can be very beneficial to your health in plenty of different ways. They are very high in vitamin C, which means that they work overtime maintaining your skin, muscles and bones. They are also a natural weight loss promoting food. Lemons may decrease your risk of cancer, and they prevent kidney stones from building up inside of the body. Lemons are also shown to improve the body's digestive health. Lemons help you absorb iron from other foods, which prevents you from getting anemia. This is due to the vitamin C and citric acid being released from the lemon when you ingest it. You can eat lemons fresh, put them inside of your water to add a little flavor, cook them with food, or make lemonade out of them.

Lime:
There are many species of limes such as, Key lime, Persian lime, Desert lime, and Makrut lime. Each species has different characteristics to them, but they are vital because of their nutritonal value. Limes are packed with vitamin C, which means that they are important to maintain your skin, muscles and bones. Limes have plenty of antioxidants in them so they promote immune system health, among other things, like absorbing iron, and preventing kidney stones. Limes are also proven to lower your risk of heart disease and certain cancers. Aside from cooking them, limes can be used in a number of ways around the house: defunking the microwave, cleaning dirty smells out of drains, taking the smell out of stinky cabinets, and act as a natural insect repellent, so you won't have to spray chemicals around the house!

Orange:
Oranges are a wonderful source of nutrients and protective plant compounds, including vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. They are very high in vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles and bones. They also prevent kidney stones and protect the body against certain types of cancers and illnesses. The flavanoid properties of the orange make it antioxidant rich. This means that oranges promote immune system health so you won't get sick as often. Whether eating or drinking oranges, you will be improving your health by including them in your diet. If you don't know what to do with your orange peels after you eat the orange, here are some ideas: use the peels to polish wood, use the peels as a sponge, or turn the peel into a candle!

Melons

Watermelon:
Watermelon is a very delicious fruit and it's also packed with nutrients, including antioxidants, and vitamins A and C. With vitamins A and C being present in watermelon, this means that it helps blood vessels, cartilage, muscle and collagen in bones, and is also important in vision, growth, cell division, reproduction and immunity. It is also chock full with antioxidants, meaning it helps promote immune system health and fights off disease & illness. Watermelon can also be used to keep you hydrated as it is 92% water. This makes it a great daily water intake fruit. You can slice the watermelon into pieces and eat it like that. You can make it into a dressing, or use the hollowed-out pieces as a fruit bowl for other food.

Cantaloupe:
Cantaloupe may be overlooked by some people, but they are a great fruit to start incorporating into your diet. They come packed with beta-carotene, which is a type of carotenoid. Carotenoids are pigments inside fruits and vegetables, which gives them their bright colors. Once consumed, beta-carotene is either converted into vitamin A, or acts as a powerful antioxidant to help fight toxins (free radicals) that attack cells in your body. Cantaloupe is also packed with vitamin C, which is necessary for blood vessels, cartilage, muscle and collagen in bones. You can eat cantaloupe in slices, or try grilling it for a nice sweet crispy snack!

Stone Fruits

Peach:
Peaches are rich in plenty of vitamins, minerals, and beneficial plant compounds. The vitamins that can be found inside of peaches are: vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles & bones; vitamin A, which helps vision, growth, cell division, reproduction and immunity; vitamin E, which acts as an antioxidant, scavenging loose electrons, also known as “free radicals”, which can damage cells; and vitamin K, which helps the body with blood clotting and helping wounds to heal. These fruits are something you should consider adding into your diet for sure! Peaches can be used to make a pie, peach cobbler, or a nice peach tea on a hot (or cold) day.

Nectarine:
Nectarines are very nutrient-packed fruits. They have vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles and bones; and vitamin B3, which will help lower cholesterol, ease arthritis, and boost brain function. Vitamin B3 is very vital in every part of your body, but can take a toll in large doses. Nectarines also have minerals such as potassium, which is necessary to help maintain normal levels of fluid inside our cells; and copper, which helps your body make red blood cells and keeps nerve cells and your immune system healthy. Nectarines can be good for making upside down cake, making a pie, or using them in a nice honey glaze to go on top of a meal.

Plum:
Plums are a great nutritious fruit. They contain vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles and bones, vitamin A, which helps vision, growth, cell division, reproduction and immunity, and vitamin K, which helps the body with blood clotting, helping wounds to heal. Plums can also help relieve constipation and are proven to lower blood sugar if it is too high. They are also rich in antioxidants, so they promote immune system health. You can use plums by adding a slice or two to your water or tea, eating them on top of a snack, such as yogurt or granola, or mixing them into a smoothie.

Cherry:
Cherries are packed with vitamins, minerals, and plant compounds with powerful positive health effects. Cherries have vitamin C in them, which is vital for maintaining your immune system and skin health. They also have potassium in them, which is needed for muscle contraction, nerve function, blood pressure regulation, and many other critical bodily processes; and can also be a good source of fiber, which helps keep your digestive system healthy by pushing beneficial gut bacteria and promoting bowel regularity. Cherries can be eaten fresh or made into a pie or cobbler.

Mango:
Mangoes and their nutrients are linked to several health benefits, such as improved immunity and digestive health. Mangoes are packed with: vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles & bones; vitamin A, which helps vision, growth, cell division, reproduction and immunity,; and vitamin K, which helps the body with blood clotting, helping wounds to heal. Mangoes can be used in a bunch of food or drinks. Even though it may look rotten, you can remove the rotten flesh from the mango and it will still be edible.

Dates:
Dates are a great source for nutrients, such as potassium, manganese, magnesium, iron, and copper. All of these minerals are responsible for forming connective tissues inside the body, maintaining fluid levels inside cells, helping make red blood cells, helping keep the immune system healthy, and energy reproduction. They are high in fiber so they promote digestive health. Dates also have a lot of antioxidants, so they are great for helping prevent diseases and illnesses. Dates are so versatile that they can be added to a multitude a foods, such as cakes, puddings, biscuits, stews, tagines, stuffings, or salads.

Tomatoes/Avacados

Tomato:
Tomatoes are confused by many as a vegetable, but they are indeed a nutrient-packed fruit. Tomatoes are a vital dietary source of an antioxidant named lycopene. Lycopene has been linked to many health benefits, such as reduced risk of heart disease and cancer. Also, tomatoes have vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles and bones; vitamin K1, which is needed for blood clotting; and vitamin B9 (folate), which aids in the production of DNA and RNA, which is the human body's genetic material. Tomatoes are also a great source of water. They can be prepared in multiple ways to eat: salsa, tomato soup, tomato sauce for spaghetti meals or chicken parmesan.

Avocado:
Avocados are very popular in the health and wellness world because they are highly nutritious and have been linked to multiple different health benefits. They pack a punch with many different types of vitamins that include: vitamin C, which means that they are vital to maintaining your skin, muscles & bones; vitamin K, which helps the body with blood clotting, helping wounds to heal; vitamin E, which is good for vision and health; and vitamins B2, B3, B5, B6, which help enzymes in our bodies do their jobs and are important for a wide range of cellular functions, like breaking down carbohydrates and transporting nutrients throughout the body. The B vitamins play a very important role in keeping our brains running properly.

Vegetables

There are 6 different categories of vegetables: Leafy Green, Cruciferous, Marrow, Root, Edible Plant Stem, Allium.

Here is a breakdown of these groups and their nutritional value.

Leafy Green

Lettuce:
Although many people will argue that lettuce has zero health benefits, it actually has many nutrients in it, making it beneficial to your health in some way. Lettuce has a few vitamins in it such as vitamin C, which is essential for the maintenance of your skin, muscles, and bones; vitamin K, which works with calcium to prevent bone fractures, and is also vital for blood clotting; and vitamin A, which is a powerful antioxidant that helps to maintain night vision and eye health. Lettuce also supports cell growth. You will also get calcium, which keeps bones and teeth strong, support muscle function, nerve function; and potassium, which reduces blood pressure by lessening the effects of salt in the diet.

Spinach:
Spinach is very beneficial to your health as is helps with eye health, reduce oxidative stress, help prevent cancer, and reduce blood pressure levels. It has many vitamins such as vitamin C, which is a powerful antioxidant that promotes skin health and immune function; and vitamin K1, which is essential for blood clotting. Also, spinach is very high in carotenoids, which your body turns into vitamin A. Spinach also has minerals such as iron, which helps create hemoglobin, which brings oxygen to your body's tissues; and calcium, which is essential for bone health and a crucial signaling molecule for your nervous system, heart, and muscles. Spinach also has a bunch of plant compounds like lutein, kaempferol, and zeaxanthin.

Swisschard (Silverbeet):
Swisschard is very useful around the world, as it does not need perfect conditions to grow, and the leaves and stalks from Swisschard provide an abundance of vitamins, minerals, and powerful plant compounds. Swisschard has plenty of vitamins like vitamin C, which is essential for the maintenance of your skin, muscles, and bones; vitamin K, which works with calcium to prevent bone fractures; and vitamin A, which is a powerful antioxidant that helps to maintain night vision and eye health. Some minerals that you will get from Swisschard are iron, which helps create hemoglobin, which brings oxygen to your body's tissues; and calcium, which is essential for bone health. Including Swisschard in your diet is a great idea and it is very easy to grow in your garden.

Cruciferous

Cabbage also contains minerals like potassium, manganese, calcium, and magnesium. These are responsible for bone health, energy reproduction, nerve function, blood clotting factors, and helping maintain fluid levels inside of cells.

Cauliflower:
Cauliflower is a delicious nutrient packed vegetable containing incredibly special plant compounds that may reduce the risk of several diseases, including heart disease and cancer. Cauliflower contains multiple vitamins, such as vitamin C, which is essential for the maintenance of your skin, muscles, and bones; vitamin K, which works with calcium to prevent bone fractures; and vitamin B6, which needed for the correct function of sugars, fats, and proteins inside the human body. Cauliflower is a great source of antioxidants, which are needed to keep the immune system healthy. You will also find minerals like potassium, phosphorus, and selenium in broccoli.

Brussel Sprout:

Celery contains vitamin C, which means that is is vital to maintaining your skin; beta carotene; and flavonoids; but there are also at least 12 additional kinds of antioxidant nutrients found in a single stalk of celery. Celery can also be a wonderful source of phytonutrients, which have been shown to reduce cases of inflammation in the digestive tract, cells, blood vessels, and organs. Allium

Onion:
Although onions are known to make you cry when you cut them, they are actually very beneficial to the human body. Onions contain antioxidants and compounds that are known to fight inflammation, decrease triglycerides, and also reduce cholesterol levels. Their potent anti-inflammatory properties will also help protect against blood clots, and reduce high blood pressure. Onions contain vitamin C, which can act as a very powerful antioxidant in your body, that protects your cells against damage caused by unstable molecules called free radicals. Onions are also rich in B vitamins, including folate (B9) & pyridoxine (B6); these play key roles in metabolism, red blood cell production, and nerve function in the body.

Garlic:
Garlic comes in cloves and most of its health benefits come from the unique compounds inside of it. Some of the compounds found in garlic are: diallyl disulfide, s-allyl cysteine, and allicin. These are responsible for anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, anticancer, cardiovascular protective, and neuroprotective properties. You will also be getting allicin, which will help prevent certain cancers and will help lower blood sugar, cholesterol, and blood pressure. Garlic also contains vitamin C, which can act as a very powerful antioxidant in your body that protects your cells against damage caused by unstable molecules called free radicals.

Shallot:
Shallots are a small, long type of onion. They are loaded with plenty of vital nutrients and plant compounds. Shallots have multiple vitamins in them such as vitamin C, which can act as a very powerful antioxidant in your body that protects your cells against damage caused by unstable molecules called free radicals; vitamin A, which helps vision, growth, cell division; and vitamin B, which help enzymes in our bodies do their jobs and are important for a wide range of cellular functions.
